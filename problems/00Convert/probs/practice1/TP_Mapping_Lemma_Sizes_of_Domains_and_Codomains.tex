%% type: short-answer
%% title: Mapping Lemma: Sizes of Domains and Codomains

For any binary relation, $\mathcal{R}: A \rightarrow B$ and subset,
$S \subset A$, let $S\mathcal{R}$ be the image of $S$ under $\mathcal{R}$.
%% , that is,
%% 
%% \begin{equation*}
%% S\mathcal{R} ::= \{ b \in B \mid \text{$s\mathcal{R}b$ for some $s \in S$} \}.
%% \end{equation*}

For example, if $\mathcal{R}$ is the doubling function with domain and
codomain equal to the real numbers, namely
\begin{equation*}
    s\mathcal{R}t\quad\text{iff}\quad t=2s,
\end{equation*}
then $\{0,3,11\}\mathcal{R} = \{0,6,22\}$, for example, and
$Z\mathcal{R}$ is the set of all even integers (here $Z$ stands for
the integers).  For any finite set, $S$, we define $|S|$ be the size
(number of elements) of~$S$.

Now assume $\mathcal{R}$ is a \textbf{total function} and $A$ is
finite.  Fill in the blanks to produce the \textbf{strongest} correct
version of the following statements:

\bparts

\ppart
$|A\mathcal{R}| \gtreqqless |B|$

\begin{solution}
is less than or equal to

since $f(A) \subset B$
\end{solution}

\ppart
If $\mathcal{R}$ is a surjection, then $|A| \gtreqqless |B|$

\begin{solution}
is greater than or equal to

by the Mapping Rule in Week 2 Notes
\end{solution}

\ppart
If $\mathcal{R}$ is a surjection, then $|A\mathcal{R}| \gtreqqless |B|$

\begin{solution}
equals
\end{solution}

\ppart
If $\mathcal{R}$ is an injection, then $|A\mathcal{R}| \gtreqqless |A|$

\begin{solution}
equals
\end{solution}

\ppart
If $\mathcal{R}$ is a bijection, then $|A| \gtreqqless |B|$

\begin{solution}
equals

by the Mapping Rule in Week 2 Notes
\end{solution}

\eparts
